Receiving a notice from the Income Tax Department can feel alarming — but most notices are routine and easily resolved if you respond correctly and on time. Here's a calm, step-by-step approach.

### Step 1: Don't panic — read carefully

Identify the section under which the notice is issued. Common ones include intimation under 143(1), a request for clarification under 139(9) for a defective return, or scrutiny under 143(2). Each has a different meaning and deadline.

### Step 2: Check the details

- Verify your name, PAN and assessment year
- Note the response deadline — this is critical
- Understand exactly what is being asked

### Step 3: Gather your documents

Pull together the relevant proofs — Form 16, bank statements, investment proofs, or whatever the notice references. Accurate documentation is your strongest response.

### Step 4: Respond on time

Most notices are answered online through the e-filing portal. Missing the deadline can lead to penalties or a best-judgment assessment — so never ignore a notice.
