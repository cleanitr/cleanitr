For any GST-registered business, missing a return deadline isn't just a paperwork slip — it triggers late fees, interest, and can even block your e-way bills. Here's what every small business should keep on its radar.

### The returns that matter most

- **GSTR-1** — details of outward supplies (sales), filed monthly or quarterly
- **GSTR-3B** — summary return with tax payment, filed monthly or quarterly under QRMP
- **GSTR-9** — annual return for eligible taxpayers

### What late filing costs you

Late fees accrue per day for each return, split across CGST and SGST, subject to a cap. On top of that, interest applies on any unpaid tax. The bigger risk is compliance rating — repeated delays can attract notices and scrutiny.

### How to stay penalty-free

- Maintain clean books month to month, not at the last minute
- Reconcile your GSTR-2B with purchase records to claim full input credit
- Set reminders a week before each due date
