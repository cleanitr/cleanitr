Every year, salaried taxpayers face the same question at filing time — should you stick with the old tax regime and its deductions, or move to the new regime with lower slab rates? The right answer depends entirely on your numbers, so let's break it down.

### The core difference

The **new regime** offers lower tax rates but removes most exemptions and deductions. The **old regime** keeps higher rates but lets you claim deductions like 80C, 80D, HRA, home loan interest and more.

### When the new regime usually wins

- You have few or no investments under 80C
- You don't claim HRA or home loan interest
- You prefer a simple, paperwork-free filing

### When the old regime usually wins

- You fully use your ₹1.5 lakh 80C limit
- You claim significant HRA or home loan interest
- You have health insurance (80D) and other deductions

A useful rule of thumb: if your total deductions cross roughly ₹3.75–4 lakh, the old regime often comes out ahead. But this varies with income level — which is exactly why you should run your own numbers.
