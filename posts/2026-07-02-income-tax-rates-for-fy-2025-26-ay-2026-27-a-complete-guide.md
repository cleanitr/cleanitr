### 1. Two Tax Regimes: New (Default) vs. Old

Since FY 2023-24, the **New Tax Regime under Section 115BAC** is the default regime for individuals, HUFs, AOPs, BOIs, and AJPs. You can still opt for the Old Regime, but you'll need to actively choose it while filing your return — and you'll lose out on the lower slab rates in exchange for being able to claim exemptions and deductions (like HRA, 80C, 80D, home loan interest, etc.).

Here's how the two compare for AY 2026-27.

#### A. New Tax Regime (Section 115BAC) — Slab Rates for AY 2026-27

| Net Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹4,00,000 | Nil |
| ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 10% |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹16,00,000 | 15% |
| ₹16,00,001 – ₹20,00,000 | 20% |
| ₹20,00,001 – ₹24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above ₹24,00,000 | 30% |

This is a meaningful widening of slabs compared to AY 2025-26 (where the Nil slab was up to ₹3,00,000 and the top 30% slab kicked in above ₹15,00,000). The government has effectively pushed more income into lower tax brackets, easing the burden on the middle class.

**Key point:** These slab rates apply uniformly — there's no separate, more generous slab structure for senior or super senior citizens under the new regime. Everyone is taxed the same way here.

#### B. Old Tax Regime — Slab Rates for AY 2026-27

The old regime slabs remain unchanged from AY 2025-26.

**Individuals (other than senior/super senior citizens):**

| Net Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹2,50,000 | Nil |
| ₹2,50,000 – ₹5,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹5,00,000 – ₹10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | 30% |

**Resident Senior Citizens (60 to under 80 years):**

| Net Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹3,00,000 | Nil |
| ₹3,00,000 – ₹5,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹5,00,000 – ₹10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | 30% |

**Resident Super Senior Citizens (80 years and above):**

| Net Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹5,00,000 | Nil |
| ₹5,00,000 – ₹10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | 30% |

Under the old regime, only resident senior and super senior citizens get the higher basic exemption limit — this benefit isn't available to non-residents, regardless of age.

### 2. Rebate Under Section 87A — A Big Relief for Middle-Income Taxpayers

Section 87A gives a full or partial rebate on tax payable, effectively making income tax-free up to a certain threshold — but the rules differ sharply between the two regimes.

- **New Regime (AY 2026-27):** A resident individual with total income up to ₹12,00,000 can claim a rebate of up to ₹60,000. This means, in effect, no tax is payable if your income doesn't exceed ₹12 lakh.
- **Old Regime:** The rebate remains capped at ₹12,500, available only if total income doesn't exceed ₹5,00,000.

**Marginal relief:** If your income crosses ₹12,00,000 (new regime) or ₹5,00,000 (old regime) by a small amount, you don't suddenly lose the entire rebate and face a full tax bill. Marginal relief ensures the additional tax you pay is capped at the amount by which your income exceeds the threshold. For example, if your income is ₹12,10,000 under the new regime, you won't pay tax as if you earned the full amount without rebate — the tax is limited to roughly the ₹10,000 excess (subject to the exact computation under the provision).

Note: this rebate is not available on income taxed at special rates, such as capital gains under Sections 111A, 112, or 112A.

### 3. Surcharge — Applicable Only Above ₹50 Lakh

Surcharge is an additional charge on the income-tax itself (not on your total income), and it only kicks in once your income crosses ₹50 lakh.

| Range of Total Income | Surcharge Rate |
|---|---|
| ₹50 Lakh – ₹1 Crore | 10% |
| ₹1 Crore – ₹2 Crore | 15% |
| ₹2 Crore – ₹5 Crore | 25% |
| Above ₹5 Crore | 37% |

**Important carve-outs:**

- The enhanced surcharge of 25%/37% does not apply to dividend income or capital gains taxed under Sections 111A, 112, 112A, and 115AD(1)(b) — for these, surcharge is capped at 15%, even if total income exceeds ₹2 crore.
- For an AOP where all members are companies, surcharge is capped at 15%.

**Marginal relief on surcharge:** This ensures that a taxpayer whose income has just crossed a surcharge threshold doesn't end up paying disproportionately more tax than someone just below that threshold. In simple terms, the extra tax + surcharge you pay can never exceed the extra income you earned beyond the threshold.

### 4. Health and Education Cess

A flat 4% cess is levied on (income-tax + surcharge), for every category of taxpayer — individuals, firms, companies, and others. There's no escaping this one; it applies uniformly.

### 5. Alternate Minimum Tax (AMT) — Relevant if You Opt for the Old Regime

If you're an individual, HUF, AOP, BOI, or AJP claiming specified deductions/exemptions under the old regime, AMT provisions may apply:

- If your tax payable (computed normally) is less than 18.5% of your "adjusted total income," you must pay tax at 18.5% of that adjusted total income instead.
- A concessional AMT rate of 9% applies to units in an International Financial Services Centre (IFSC) deriving income solely in convertible foreign exchange.
- Good news if you're on the new regime: AMT simply doesn't apply if you've opted for the Section 115BAC (new) regime.

### 6. Quick Comparison: New Regime vs. Old Regime (AY 2026-27)

| Feature | New Regime (115BAC) | Old Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Basic exemption | ₹4,00,000 | ₹2,50,000 (₹3L/₹5L for seniors) |
| Top rate (30%) kicks in above | ₹24,00,000 | ₹10,00,000 |
| Section 87A rebate limit | Income up to ₹12,00,000 (rebate up to ₹60,000) | Income up to ₹5,00,000 (rebate up to ₹12,500) |
| Deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, home loan interest, etc.) | Mostly not allowed | Allowed |
| Default regime | Yes | No — must be opted for |
| AMT applicability | Not applicable | Applicable if claiming specified deductions |

**Which one should you choose?** As a rule of thumb: if you don't have significant deductions to claim (large home loan interest, heavy 80C investments, HRA, etc.), the new regime will almost always save you more tax because of the wider slabs and the higher 87A rebate threshold. If you have substantial deductions — especially a home loan on a self-occupied property plus full 80C utilization — it's worth running the numbers under both regimes before deciding. Most tax-filing utilities and calculators will show you both computations side by side.

### 7. Other Categories at a Glance

For completeness, here's how other entities are taxed for AY 2026-27:

- **Partnership Firms (including LLPs):** Flat 30%, plus 12% surcharge (if income exceeds ₹1 crore) and 4% cess.
- **Domestic Companies:** 25% if turnover in FY 2023-24 didn't exceed ₹400 crore, else 30%. Special concessional rates apply if the company has opted for Sections 115BA (25%), 115BAA (22%), or 115BAB (15%).
- **Foreign Companies:** 35% on most income; 50% on certain royalty/technical fee income from older agreements (pre-1976).
- **Co-operative Societies:** Slab-based (10%/20%/30%), with concessional flat rates of 22% (Section 115BAD) or 15% on manufacturing income (Section 115BAE) available on opting in.
- **Local Authorities:** Flat 30%, plus applicable surcharge and cess.

### 8. Illustrative Example (New Regime, AY 2026-27)

Suppose your total taxable income is ₹15,00,000.

| Slab | Rate | Tax |
|---|---|---|
| Up to ₹4,00,000 | Nil | ₹0 |
| ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 | 5% | ₹20,000 |
| ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 10% | ₹40,000 |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹15,00,000 | 15% | ₹45,000 |
| **Total tax before cess** | | **₹1,05,000** |
| Add: Health & Education Cess (4%) | | ₹4,200 |
| **Total Tax Payable** | | **₹1,09,200** |

Since income exceeds ₹12,00,000, no Section 87A rebate applies here — the rebate only fully wipes out tax if income is at or below ₹12,00,000.

### Key Takeaways

- The New Regime is now the default — you must explicitly opt for the Old Regime if you want it.
- The ₹12,00,000 rebate threshold under the new regime is a major relief for salaried and middle-income taxpayers.
- Surcharge only matters if you're earning above ₹50 lakh — and marginal relief protects you from being disproportionately taxed right after crossing a threshold.
- Always compute your tax liability under both regimes before filing, especially if you have significant deductions.
