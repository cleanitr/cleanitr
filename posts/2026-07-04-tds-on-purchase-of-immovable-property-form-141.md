The Income-tax Act, 2025 has replaced the Income-tax Act, 1961 with effect from **1st April 2026**. The familiar provision requiring a property buyer to deduct tax at source — earlier **Section 194-IA** — is now housed in **Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. 3(i)]**, and the well-known **Form 26QB** has been replaced by the new consolidated **Form 141 (Schedule B)** under the Income-tax Rules, 2026.

> **In one line:** Buying a flat, plot, house or commercial property worth ₹50 lakh or more (by consideration or stamp duty value)? You — the buyer — must deduct 1% TDS, deposit it through Form 141 within 30 days from the end of the month of deduction, and issue a TDS certificate to the seller.

## Legal Framework at a Glance

The core scheme remains the same as under the old law: **any person purchasing immovable property (other than agricultural land) from a resident seller must deduct TDS @ 1%** where the consideration or the stamp duty value (SDV) is **₹50 lakh or more**. What has changed materially is the compliance framework — section numbers, form numbers and terminology (a single unified **"Tax Year"** replaces FY/AY).

<span id="sec-property"></span>

## What is Immovable Property?

For Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. 3(i)], **"immovable property" means any land (other than agricultural land) or any building, or part of a building** — mirroring the Explanation to the old Section 194-IA.

**What falls within the net:**

- **Residential property** — flats, apartments, independent houses, villas, builder floors (including under-construction units purchased from a builder)
- **Commercial property** — shops, offices, showrooms, industrial units, warehouses
- **Land** — residential plots, commercial plots, industrial land and *urban* agricultural land (see next section)
- **Part of a building** — a floor, a wing, or an undivided share
- **Rights in or with respect to such property** — leasehold, tenancy/occupancy rights and similar interests are generally regarded as covered

### Composite consideration — what counts as "consideration"

Under the expansive definition in Section 26(9) of the 2025 Act, "consideration for transfer" includes not only the basic sale price but **all incidental charges** — club membership fee, car parking fee, electricity and water facility charges, maintenance fee, advance fee and similar charges. Where the property is transferred together with **machinery, plant, furniture or fittings**, such items are also treated as part of the immovable property.

> **Practical caution:** Builders often split the price into "basic cost" plus PLC, parking, club charges, etc. The ₹50 lakh threshold and the 1% TDS must be tested on the **total package**, not just the basic sale price.

<span id="sec-agri"></span>

## What is Covered? Meaning of Agricultural Land

The exclusion is only for **rural agricultural land**. The Act does not exempt every piece of land on which farming is done — **urban agricultural land is fully covered** by TDS.

Agricultural land qualifies as **rural** (and hence outside TDS) only if it is **not situated**:

- **(a)** within a municipality/cantonment board having a population of **10,000 or more**; or
- **(b)** within the following **aerial (straight-line) distance** from the local limits of such municipality/cantonment:

| Population of the municipality / cantonment | Aerial distance within which land is "urban" |
| --- | --- |
| More than 10,000 but up to 1,00,000 | Up to 2 km |
| More than 1,00,000 but up to 10,00,000 | Up to 6 km |
| More than 10,00,000 | Up to 8 km |

*Population* means the population as per the last preceding census whose figures were published before the first day of the tax year.

> **Summary of coverage:** TDS applies to land, building or part of building — residential, commercial, industrial, plots and urban agricultural land. TDS does **not** apply to rural agricultural land, to deals where both consideration and SDV are below ₹50 lakh, and to compulsory acquisition under any law (separately covered by Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. 3(iii)] — successor of Section 194LA).

<span id="sec-buyer"></span>

## Who is the Buyer (Transferee)?

The **buyer** is **any person** responsible for paying consideration to a resident seller for the transfer of immovable property. The entire obligation — deduct, deposit, report, certify — rests on the buyer.

- **Every class of buyer is covered** — individual, HUF, firm, LLP, company, AOP/BOI, trust — with no turnover or audit-based exemption
- **Resident and non-resident buyers alike** — even an NRI buyer purchasing from a resident seller must deduct
- **No TAN required** — under Section 397(1)(c)(i) of the 2025 Act, the buyer's **PAN** is sufficient; Form 141 is a PAN-based filing
- **Joint buyers** — each buyer deducts TDS on his/her share and files a **separate Form 141**
- **Purpose immaterial** — applies whether the property is a capital asset or stock-in-trade, and even if the property is situated outside India (seller being resident)

<span id="sec-seller"></span>

## Who is the Seller (Transferor)?

The seller must be a **resident** in India — the seller's residential status (not the buyer's) decides which provision applies:

| Status of Seller | ITA 2025 Provision | Old Act Equivalent | Broad Position |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Resident** | Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. 3(i)] | Sec. 194-IA | 1% TDS if consideration/SDV ≥ ₹50 lakh; Form 141 (PAN-based, no TAN) |
| **Non-resident (NRI/foreign)** | Section 393(2) [Table: Sl. No. 17] | Sec. 195 | Higher capital-gains-linked rates + surcharge & cess, no ₹50 lakh threshold, TAN required, quarterly statement — Form 141 **cannot** be used |

The buyer must obtain and verify the seller's **PAN**; if PAN is not furnished or is inoperative, TDS is deductible at a **higher rate of 20%** under Section 397(2) (old Section 206AA).

<span id="sec-requirement"></span>

## Requirement &amp; Conditions for Deducting TDS

TDS is attracted when **all** of the following are satisfied:

1. There is a **transfer of immovable property** (land other than rural agricultural land, or building or part of a building);
2. The **seller is a resident**;
3. The **consideration OR the stamp duty value** is **₹50,00,000 or more** (if *both* are below ₹50 lakh, no TDS); and
4. The buyer makes **payment or credit** of such consideration (including advances/instalments).

### Multiple buyers / multiple sellers

Per Note 1 to Table Sl. No. 3 of Section 393(1), where there is more than one buyer or seller, the ₹50 lakh limit is tested on the **aggregate amount paid by all buyers to all sellers** for the property — i.e., **deal-wise, not person-wise**. Splitting the deal among joint buyers/sellers so that each share falls below ₹50 lakh does **not** escape TDS.

### Time of deduction

TDS must be deducted at **credit or payment, whichever is earlier**. For instalment payments (typical in builder bookings), TDS is deducted on **each instalment**, including booking/advance amounts.

<span id="sec-rate"></span>

## Rate of TDS &amp; Time Limit to Deposit

| Particulars | Position under ITA 2025 |
| --- | --- |
| Rate of TDS | **1%** of the consideration **or** the stamp duty value, **whichever is higher** |
| Seller's PAN not furnished / inoperative | **20%** under Section 397(2) (old Sec. 206AA) |
| Surcharge / cess | Not applicable for resident seller — flat 1% |
| Time of deduction | Credit or payment, **whichever is earlier** (each instalment) |
| Time limit to deposit &amp; report | **Form 141 (challan-cum-statement)** within **30 days from the end of the month** of deduction [Rule 218(3)(b), IT Rules 2026] |
| Separate TDS return? | **No** — Form 141 is the challan *and* the statement |
| TAN requirement | **Not required** — PAN-based [Sec. 397(1)(c)(i)] |

> **Illustration of the due date:** TDS deducted on 12th July 2026 → Form 141 must be filed and tax deposited on or before **30th August 2026**.

<span id="sec-form141"></span>

## Form 141 — Which Transactions are Covered?

Yes — **Form 141 is the prescribed form for TDS on property purchase**, and it also covers three other PAN-based TDS transactions. It is a single consolidated **challan-cum-statement** replacing the four old forms — **26QB, 26QC, 26QD and 26QE** — through four schedules:

| Schedule | Nature of Transaction | ITA 2025 Reference | Old Form (ITA 1961) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Schedule A** | TDS on rent paid by Individual/HUF | 393(1), Table Sl. No. 2(i) | Form 26QC (Sec. 194-IB) |
| **Schedule B** | **TDS on transfer of immovable property** | 393(1), Table Sl. No. 3(i) | **Form 26QB (Sec. 194-IA)** |
| **Schedule C** | TDS on payments by Individual/HUF to contractors/professionals | 393(1), Table Sl. No. 6(ii) | Form 26QD (Sec. 194M) |
| **Schedule D** | TDS on transfer of Virtual Digital Assets by Individual/HUF | 393(1), Table Sl. No. 8(vi) | Form 26QE (Sec. 194S) |

**Key features:** one transaction type (schedule) per form; one form per deductor (joint buyers file separately); multiple sellers allowed in one form only if all are of the same category (corporate/non-corporate) and same month of deduction; **resident sellers only**; filed **online only through PAN login**; corrections only via the **TRACES portal**.

<span id="sec-steps"></span>

## Steps to File Form 141 (Schedule B — Immovable Property)

1. **Log in with PAN** — Visit *www.incometax.gov.in* and log in to the e-filing portal with the buyer's PAN credentials.
2. **Navigate to e-Pay Tax** — Go to *e-File → e-Pay Tax* and select **"Income Tax Act, 2025"**.
3. **Select New Payment → Form 141** — Click **Proceed** on the tile "Form 141 [Challan-cum-statement of deduction of tax under section 393(1)]".
4. **Choose the Deductee Type** — Select **Corporate Deductee** if the seller is a company (e.g., a builder company), else **Non-Corporate Deductee**.
5. **Select Schedule B** — "TDS on transfer of immovable property under section 393(1)" (earlier known as Form 26QB).
6. **Fill in Transaction Details** — Buyer's and seller's PAN/name/address, property details, date of agreement, total consideration, stamp duty value, payment type (lump sum/instalment), amount paid, date and month of deduction, and TDS @ 1%. Click **Save** — entries remain editable in a table.
7. **Continue to Payment Mode** — Select net banking / debit card / over the counter / NEFT-RTGS / payment gateway.
8. **Preview &amp; Pay** — Verify everything on the "Preview and Make Payment" page (use **Edit** for corrections), then click **Pay Now**.
9. **Download the Challan Receipt** — A confirmation SMS/e-mail follows; filed forms are visible under *e-File → e-Pay Tax → Income Tax Act, 2025 → Payment History*.
10. **Issue the TDS Certificate** — Register/log in on **TRACES** as "Taxpayer", generate the certificate (successor of old Form 16B) and hand it to the seller.

> **Remember:** One Form 141 covers only one month of deduction — for instalments deducted in different months, file a separate Form 141 for each month, each within 30 days from that month's end.

<span id="sec-certificate"></span>

## TDS Certificate to the Seller

After depositing the TDS, the buyer issues a **TDS certificate** under **Section 395 of the ITA 2025 read with Rule 132 of the Income-tax Rules, 2026** — the functional equivalent of the old **Form 16B** — generated from the **TRACES portal**. The seller uses it to claim TDS credit in the return (the credit also reflects in the seller's Form 26AS/AIS).

<span id="sec-consequences"></span>

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

| Default | Consequence | ITA 2025 | ITA 1961 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Failure to deduct | Interest @ **1% p.m.** from date deductible to date of deduction; assessee-in-default risk | Sec. 398(3)/398(1) | Sec. 201(1A)(i)/201(1) |
| Deducted but deposited late | Interest @ **1.5% p.m.** from date of deduction to date of deposit | Sec. 398(3) | Sec. 201(1A)(ii) |
| Late filing of Form 141 | **₹200 per day** (capped at TDS amount; Budget 2026 has proposed an overall ₹1,00,000 cap) | Sec. 427 | Sec. 234E |
| Non-filing / incorrect statement | Penalty **₹10,000 – ₹1,00,000** (relief if tax, interest &amp; fee paid and statement filed within the prescribed window) | Sec. 461 | Sec. 271H |
| Failure to deposit with Government | **Prosecution** — rigorous imprisonment 3 months to 7 years, with fine | Sec. 476 | Sec. 276B |
| Relief where seller has paid tax | Buyer not treated as assessee-in-default if resident seller filed return, included the income, paid tax, and CA certificate furnished | Sec. 398(2) | Proviso to Sec. 201(1) (Form 26A) |

> **Registrar linkage:** Sub-registrars routinely insist on TDS challan proof for registering high-value conveyances, and the department receives SFT reporting of property deals of ₹30 lakh+ — non-deduction is easily detected and triggers system-generated notices to the buyer.

## Detailed Worked Example

### Example 1 — Instalment purchase from a resident seller

**Facts:** Mr. Aman (resident buyer) purchases a flat in Gurugram from Mr. Verma (resident seller) for **₹85,00,000**; stamp duty value **₹84,00,000**. Payments during Tax Year 2026-27:

| Instalment | Date of Payment | Amount Paid | TDS @ 1% | Net Paid to Seller | Form 141 Due Date |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Booking amount | 20-05-2026 | ₹10,00,000 | ₹10,000 | ₹9,90,000 | 30-06-2026 |
| Second instalment | 15-07-2026 | ₹40,00,000 | ₹40,000 | ₹39,60,000 | 30-08-2026 |
| Final (registration) | 10-09-2026 | ₹35,00,000 | ₹35,000 | ₹34,65,000 | 30-10-2026 |
| **Total** | | **₹85,00,000** | **₹85,000** | **₹84,15,000** | |

**Analysis:**

- **Applicability:** Consideration (₹85 lakh) ≥ ₹50 lakh, property is a flat, seller is resident → Section 393(1) [Table Sl. No. 3(i)] applies.
- **Base for TDS:** Higher of consideration (₹85,00,000) and SDV (₹84,00,000) = ₹85,00,000 → total TDS **₹85,000**, deducted instalment-wise.
- **Compliance:** TDS falls in three different months (May, July, September 2026) → **three separate Forms 141 (Schedule B)**, each within 30 days from the respective month-end, plus TRACES certificate to Mr. Verma after each filing. No TAN needed.

**If the second instalment's TDS is deposited late** — say the ₹40,000 deducted on 15-07-2026 is deposited on **15-09-2026** instead of 30-08-2026:

- **Interest u/s 398(3):** 1.5% per month or part thereof from 15-07-2026 to 15-09-2026 = 3 months → ₹40,000 × 1.5% × 3 = **₹1,800**
- **Late fee u/s 427:** ₹200/day × 16 days (31-08 to 15-09) = **₹3,200** (within the TDS-amount cap)
- Total extra outflow ≈ **₹5,000**, apart from penalty exposure u/s 461 for prolonged delay.

### Example 2 — Joint buyers: threshold on aggregate value

Mr. Raj and Mrs. Raj jointly buy a flat for **₹90,00,000** (SDV ₹92,00,000) from a resident builder company, paying ₹45,00,000 each.

- Individually each pays below ₹50 lakh, but the threshold is tested on the **aggregate deal value** (₹90 lakh / SDV ₹92 lakh) → **TDS applies**.
- TDS base = higher figure ₹92,00,000 → total TDS **₹92,000**, i.e., **₹46,000 each** in proportion to their shares.
- Each spouse files a **separate Form 141 (Schedule B)** selecting **Corporate Deductee** (seller is a company), reporting the total property value and own share of payment/TDS.

## Transition Rules: Form 26QB vs Form 141 — Which Form to Use?

The governing Act — and hence the correct form — depends on **when the TDS trigger (credit or payment, whichever earlier) occurs**, not on the agreement or registration date:

| Date of payment/credit (whichever earlier) | Governing Law | Form |
| --- | --- | --- |
| On or before **31st March 2026** | ITA 1961 (Sec. 194-IA) | Form 26QB |
| On or after **1st April 2026** | ITA 2025 [Sec. 393(1), Table 3(i)] | Form 141 — Schedule B |

**Example:** Agreement executed in February 2026; ₹20 lakh paid in March 2026 and ₹70 lakh in June 2026. The March payment goes in **Form 26QB** (old Act); the June payment goes in **Form 141** (new Act). Corrections to old 26QB filings continue in the old format on TRACES even after 01.04.2026.

## FAQs

**Q1. Does the buyer need a TAN?**
No — Section 397(1)(c)(i) exempts property buyers from TAN. Form 141 is filed with the buyer's PAN login.

**Q2. TDS on stamp duty value or actual price?**
On whichever is **higher**. The ₹50 lakh threshold is also tested against both — TDS applies if either figure is ₹50 lakh or more.

**Q3. Price ₹48 lakh but SDV ₹52 lakh — TDS required?**
Yes, because SDV ≥ ₹50 lakh. TDS = 1% of ₹52 lakh.

**Q4. Two joint buyers paying ₹40 lakh each for an ₹80 lakh flat?**
TDS applies — the threshold is tested on the aggregate (₹80 lakh). Each buyer deducts 1% on own share and files a separate Form 141.

**Q5. Seller is an NRI — can I use Form 141?**
No. Form 141 is only for resident sellers. For NRI sellers, Section 393(2) [Table Sl. No. 17] (old Sec. 195) applies — higher rates, TAN required, quarterly statement. Take professional advice for such deals.

**Q6. Is agricultural land always exempt?**
Only **rural** agricultural land (per the municipality-population and 2/6/8-km aerial-distance test). Urban agricultural land attracts TDS.

**Q7. Can the seller get lower/nil TDS?**
Yes — by obtaining a certificate from the Assessing Officer under Section 395(1) (old Sec. 197).

**Q8. How do I correct a filed Form 141?**
Not on the e-filing portal — register as "Taxpayer" on **TDS-TRACES** and file the correction there.
