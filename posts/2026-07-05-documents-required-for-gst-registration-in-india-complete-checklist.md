GST registration is the process through which a business obtains its unique 15-digit **GST Identification Number (GSTIN)** from the tax authorities. The entire process is **completely online and free of cost** on the official GST portal ([www.gst.gov.in](https://www.gst.gov.in)) — yet the single most common reason applications get delayed or rejected is incorrect, unclear or mismatched documents. This guide gives you the complete, updated checklist so your application sails through, usually within **3–7 working days**.

### 1. Who Needs GST Registration?

GST registration is **mandatory** in the following cases:

| Category | Threshold / Condition |
|---|---|
| Supplier of goods (normal category states) | Annual turnover above **₹40 lakh** |
| Supplier of services | Annual turnover above **₹20 lakh** |
| Special category states | **₹10–20 lakh** depending on state and nature of supply |
| Inter-state supply of taxable goods | Mandatory, irrespective of turnover |
| Sellers on e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Flipkart, own website) | Mandatory, irrespective of turnover |
| Casual taxable persons (exhibition/stall vendors) | Mandatory |
| Non-resident taxable persons | Mandatory |
| Persons liable under Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM) | Mandatory |
| Persons required to deduct TDS / collect TCS under GST | Mandatory |

Businesses below the threshold may also opt for **voluntary registration** to claim input tax credit and improve business credibility.

### 2. Core Documents Required for All Applicants

Regardless of your business structure, every GST application is built on these:

1. **PAN card** — of the business entity (or the individual, for a proprietorship). The legal name on the PAN must exactly match the name entered on the GST portal.
2. **Aadhaar card** — of the proprietor, partners or directors. Aadhaar authentication is now mandatory for new registrations.
3. **Photograph** — passport-size photo of the proprietor / partners / directors / Karta / authorised signatory (JPEG, max 100 KB).
4. **Proof of business address** — for the principal place of business (Section 4 below).
5. **Bank account proof** — cancelled cheque or bank statement/passbook extract (Section 5 below).
6. **Proof of business constitution** — partnership deed, certificate of incorporation, etc.
7. **Authorisation document** — letter of authorisation or board resolution appointing the authorised signatory, where applicable.
8. **Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)** — mandatory for companies and LLPs at submission.
9. **Mobile number and email ID** — for OTP verification.

### 3. Documents Based on Business Constitution

#### A. Sole Proprietorship / Individual

- PAN card and Aadhaar card of the proprietor
- Photograph of the proprietor (JPEG, max 100 KB)
- Address proof of the place of business
- Bank account details

*A proprietorship has no separate legal identity, so the proprietor's personal PAN is used. A home address can be used as the business address with valid proof.*

#### B. Partnership Firm

- PAN card of the firm + **partnership deed**
- PAN and Aadhaar of all partners
- Photographs of all partners and the authorised signatory
- Address proof of partners (Aadhaar / passport / driving licence / voter ID)
- Letter of authorisation for the authorised signatory + their Aadhaar
- Address proof of the principal place of business
- Bank account details

#### C. Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

Everything listed for a partnership firm, **plus**:

- **Certificate of incorporation** issued by the MCA
- LLP agreement
- Board resolution / proof of appointment of authorised signatory
- Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) of the authorised signatory

#### D. Private / Public Limited Company

- PAN card of the company + **certificate of incorporation** (MCA)
- Memorandum and Articles of Association (MOA & AOA)
- PAN and Aadhaar of all directors + their address proof and photographs
- PAN and Aadhaar of the authorised signatory (must be an Indian resident, even for foreign companies)
- **Board resolution** appointing the authorised signatory
- Address proof of the principal place of business
- Bank account details
- Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) of the authorised signatory

#### E. Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)

- PAN card of the HUF
- PAN, Aadhaar and photograph of the **Karta**
- Address proof of the principal place of business
- Bank account details

#### F. Society, Trust, Club or NGO

- PAN card + **registration certificate** of the society/trust
- PAN and photographs of promoters / trustees / managing committee members
- Committee resolution + PAN and Aadhaar of the authorised signatory
- Address proof of the registered office
- Bank account details

### 4. Address Proof of Principal Place of Business

The documents depend on whether the premises are **owned, rented or used with consent**. Upload **any one**, as applicable:

**If the premises are owned:**

- Latest property tax receipt, or
- Municipal khata copy, or
- Recent electricity bill, or
- Ownership deed / legal ownership document

**If rented or leased:**

- Valid rent / lease agreement, **along with** any one ownership document of the lessor (electricity bill, property tax receipt or khata copy)
- If no rent agreement exists: an affidavit to that effect along with a document supporting possession (such as an electricity bill in the applicant's name)

**If shared / used with consent (e.g., family-owned property):**

- **Consent letter / NOC** from the owner, **along with** any one ownership proof of the owner

**If located in an SEZ:** certificates or documents issued by the Government of India relating to the SEZ status.

*For every additional place of business, similar proof must be provided.*

<a id="gstdoc-5"></a>

### 5. Bank Account Proof

Upload **any one** of the following (JPEG or PDF, max 100 KB):

- **Cancelled cheque** with the account holder's name printed, or
- Extract of the **bank passbook / statement** showing the first and last pages (account number, IFSC, branch and holder details)

Bank details can also be furnished after registration is granted, at first login — but keeping them ready speeds up compliance.

### 6. Documents for Special Types of Registration

| Registration Type | Additional Requirements |
|---|---|
| Composition Scheme dealer | Same as normal taxpayer; option exercised online |
| Casual Taxable Person | Standard documents + estimated turnover + advance tax deposit |
| Non-Resident Taxable Person | Passport / tax ID of foreign entity, Indian authorised signatory with PAN, Indian bank account, advance tax deposit |
| Input Service Distributor (ISD) | Standard documents + ISD authorisation proof |
| TDS Deductor (Government bodies) | TAN, proof of appointment of DDO, authorisation letter |
| TCS Collector (E-commerce operator) | Standard entity documents + platform details |
| E-commerce sellers | No special documents — standard set as per constitution + e-commerce business details |

### 7. File Format & Size Requirements on the GST Portal

| Document | Format | Maximum Size |
|---|---|---|
| Photographs | JPEG | 100 KB |
| Bank account proof | JPEG / PDF | 100 KB |
| Address proof documents | JPEG / PDF | 100 KB – 500 KB (varies) |
| Constitution proof (deed / incorporation certificate) | JPEG / PDF | 1 MB |
| Board resolution / authorisation letter | JPEG / PDF | 100 KB |

Scan documents clearly — blurry or cropped uploads invite queries. Compress files within limits *without* losing readability, and keep a valid DSC ready if you're a company or LLP.

<a id="gstdoc-8"></a>

### 8. Aadhaar Authentication Requirement

- The applicant (proprietor / partner / director / Karta / authorised signatory) must complete **Aadhaar e-KYC authentication** via OTP.
- Applications with successful Aadhaar authentication are processed **faster** — often within about 7 working days.
- If authentication is skipped or fails, the application is routed for **physical verification** of the business premises by a GST officer, which can stretch the timeline to 30 days or more.

<a id="gstdoc-9"></a>

### 9. Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Name mismatch** — the legal name must exactly match PAN records.
- **Address mismatch** — the proof uploaded must match the address entered.
- **Blurry or partial scans** — always upload clear, complete documents.
- **Wrong file size/format** — check PDF/JPEG limits before uploading.
- **Missing NOC/consent letter** — needed whenever the premises aren't owned by the applicant.
- **Ignoring clarification notices** — respond to Form GST REG-03 queries within the given time (typically 7 working days), else the application is rejected.
- **Outdated documents** — use recent electricity bills / tax receipts.

### 10. FAQs

**Is there any government fee for GST registration?**
No. Registration on www.gst.gov.in is completely free; only professional fees apply if you hire a consultant.

**Are PAN and Aadhaar both mandatory?**
Yes — PAN for the entity/individual, and Aadhaar authentication for the proprietor, partners, directors or authorised signatory.

**Can I use my home address?**
Yes. Home-based businesses can register with valid ownership proof, or a consent letter/NOC if the property belongs to a family member.

**How long does registration take?**
Typically 3–7 working days with successful Aadhaar authentication and correct documents; longer if physical verification is triggered.

**Do e-commerce sellers need special documents?**
No — the standard set as per business constitution, plus e-commerce details. But registration is mandatory for them regardless of turnover.
